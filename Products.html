Choosing The Right Phone For Yourself

Your smartphones are probably one of the most important gadgets in your life. They not only help you to stay connected to your family and friends but also help you carry your work and leisure, such as music, movies and more, wherever you go. Hence, it becomes essential that you settle nothing short of best. Not just the look of the mobile phone becomes an essential factor but also the latest features. With a plethora of smartphones available in the market each month, you might be looking out for something that suits your style and requirement. Below are the few things which you should keep in mind before buying your new smartphone.

Build Quality- Durability of a smartphone is of high importance. Generally, you will get two types of builds- metal and plastic. Also, make sure your phone is water-resistant and good in aesthetics if you are particular about the look of your phone.

Get The Right Screen Size-You can opt for mobile phones with a screen smaller than 5.5 inches. Go for a bigger screen size if you are more into watching videos and playing games. For heavy usage, go for bigger screens ranging from 5.5 inches to 6 inches.

Processor- A good processor will offer smooth gaming experience, quicker photo editing and faster open times for apps. If you do a lot of things with your smartphone like editing images, videos, documents online, play games then, opting for smartphones with Snapdragon 652 or Snapdragon 820/821 will make multitasking smoother. For simple usage, MediaTek processors will do the trick for you.

RAM-High capacity RAM is essential for multitasking. While 2GB RAM is a standard option, 4GB RAMs are considered premium feature and 6GB RAM is found in high-tech phones like those of Galaxy Note 8.

Battery Life-Every smartphone user not only wants their phone to charge faster but lasting longer on one charge. If you have a normal phone usage, then cell phones with 3000 mAh battery will be good to go and for more intense use can consider handsets with 3500 mAh battery or above.

Camera-For top camera performance, you don’t have to run after the higher number of pixels. Other things you need to take into consideration include a bright aperture, lenses & zoom, ISO control, autofocus and more.
